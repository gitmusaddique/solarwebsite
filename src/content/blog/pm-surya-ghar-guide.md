## What is PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana?

Launched by Prime Minister Narendra Modi on February 13, 2024, PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana is the Government of India's most ambitious rooftop solar programme to date. With a total outlay of ₹75,021 crore, this scheme aims to:

- Install rooftop solar on **1 crore households** across India
- Provide **300 units of free electricity** per month to beneficiary families
- Generate **30 GW of solar capacity**
- Create **17 lakh direct jobs** in the solar sector

## Who Can Apply?

The scheme is available to all residential electricity consumers in India. You qualify if:

- You are an **Indian citizen with valid Aadhaar**
- Your property has an **existing electricity connection from DISCOM**
- You have **adequate roof space** (minimum 10 sq m for 1 kW)
- Your roof is **structurally sound** and shadow-free for most of the day
- You use an **MNRE-empanelled installer**

## Understanding the Subsidy Structure

The scheme provides Central Financial Assistance (CFA) based on system size:

| System Capacity | CFA (Subsidy) |
|----------------|---------------|
| Up to 1 kW | ₹30,000 |
| 1 kW to 2 kW | ₹60,000 |
| 2 kW to 3 kW | ₹78,000 |
| Above 3 kW | ₹78,000 (maximum) |

### State-Level Top-Up Subsidies

Many states offer additional subsidies on top of the central subsidy:

- **Maharashtra**: Additional ₹10,000 for residential consumers
- **Gujarat**: Up to ₹40,000 additional for PM Surya Ghar applicants
- **Rajasthan**: 40% state-level CFA for select categories
- **Tamil Nadu**: Concessional financing at 5% interest
- **Uttar Pradesh**: Additional ₹15,000 through state solar mission

## Step-by-Step Application Process

### Step 1: Register on the National Portal

Visit **pmsuryagarh.gov.in** and register with your:
- Mobile number (linked to Aadhaar)
- State and electricity distribution company
- Electricity consumer number

### Step 2: Select a Vendor

Choose from the portal's list of empanelled vendors in your area. **Solarelites is empanelled** in Maharashtra, Gujarat, Rajasthan, Tamil Nadu, and Karnataka.

Your vendor will conduct a site inspection and submit the installation design.

### Step 3: DISCOM Approval

Your DISCOM reviews and approves the design. This typically takes 7–15 working days. Our team tracks this and follows up on your behalf.

### Step 4: Installation

Once approved, your vendor installs the solar system (1–3 days for residential).

### Step 5: Net Meter Application

After installation, your vendor applies for a net meter. DISCOM installs the net meter within 30 days of application.

### Step 6: Subsidy Disbursement

After net meter installation, DISCOM generates a commissioning certificate. The subsidy amount is directly credited to your Aadhaar-linked bank account within 30 working days.

## Documents Required

Prepare these documents in advance to speed up the process:

1. **Aadhaar Card** (original + copy)
2. **PAN Card**
3. **Latest Electricity Bill** (showing consumer number)
4. **Bank Account Details** (Aadhaar-linked account passbook/statement)
5. **Property Documents** (sale deed, property tax receipt)
6. **Passport-size Photographs** (2 copies)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Don't pay vendors before DISCOM approval** — legitimate installers don't ask for full payment upfront
2. **Verify vendor empanelment** — always confirm on the national portal
3. **Don't choose size just for subsidy** — right-size your system for your actual consumption
4. **Save all receipts** — subsidy audit may require documentation
5. **Apply for net metering** — don't forget this step for maximum savings
