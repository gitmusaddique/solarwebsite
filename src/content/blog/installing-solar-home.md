## Why Every Indian Homeowner Should Consider Solar in 2025

India's solar revolution is no longer a distant promise — it's happening right now, on rooftops across every state. With electricity tariffs rising 6–8% annually and the central government offering subsidies up to ₹78,000 under the PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana, the economics of residential solar have never been stronger.

In this comprehensive guide, we walk you through every aspect of going solar — from assessing your roof to claiming your subsidy.

## Understanding Your Energy Needs

Before anything else, you need to understand your home's electricity consumption. Pull out your last 12 electricity bills and note:

- **Average monthly units consumed** (in kWh)
- **Peak summer and winter consumption**
- **Time-of-use patterns** — when does your household consume most energy?

A typical Indian middle-class home consumes between 200–600 units per month. Your solar system size should be designed to offset most of this consumption.

## Choosing the Right Solar System Type

### On-Grid Solar Systems

On-grid systems are the most popular and cost-effective choice for urban homes with reliable grid connectivity. They:

- Generate electricity during daylight hours
- Export surplus power to the grid (net metering)
- Automatically shut down during grid outages (for safety)
- Qualify for PM Surya Ghar subsidy

**Best for:** Urban and semi-urban homes with stable grid supply

### Off-Grid Solar Systems

Off-grid systems are fully independent of the electricity grid. They include battery storage and are ideal for areas with frequent power cuts.

- Provide 24/7 electricity independence
- Higher upfront cost due to batteries
- No net metering benefits
- Ideal for rural areas

**Best for:** Rural areas, farms, locations with poor grid reliability

### Hybrid Solar Systems

The best of both worlds — hybrid systems connect to the grid AND have battery backup. They store surplus energy in batteries first, then export remaining surplus to the grid.

- Power during grid outages from batteries
- Net metering when batteries are full
- Higher cost but maximum flexibility

**Best for:** Homes with frequent power cuts who still want net metering benefits

## The PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana

The Government of India's PM Surya Ghar scheme is transformational. Under this scheme:

| System Size | Central Subsidy |
|-------------|----------------|
| 1 kW | ₹30,000 |
| 2 kW | ₹60,000 |
| 3 kW | ₹78,000 |
| Above 3 kW | ₹78,000 (capped) |

### Eligibility Requirements

1. Must be an Indian residential electricity consumer
2. Valid Aadhaar card linked to bank account
3. Existing electricity connection from DISCOM
4. Roof space sufficient for solar installation
5. Must use an MNRE-empanelled installer (Solarelites is empanelled)

### Application Process

The entire process happens online through the National Portal for Rooftop Solar (pmsuryagarh.gov.in). At Solarelites, we handle the entire application on your behalf.

## How to Calculate Your Payback Period

Here's a simple formula:

**Annual Savings = System Size (kW) × Solar Hours Per Day × 365 × Electricity Tariff**

For a 3 kW system in Maharashtra:
- Annual generation: 3 × 5.5 × 365 = 6,022 kWh
- Annual savings at ₹8/kWh: ₹48,180
- Gross system cost: ₹1,95,000
- After ₹78,000 subsidy: ₹1,17,000
- Payback period: 2.4 years

After payback, your solar system generates **free electricity for 20+ more years**.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How much roof area do I need for solar panels?**
A: Each kW of solar panels requires approximately 10 square meters (100 sq ft) of shadow-free roof space. A 3 kW system needs about 30 sq m.

**Q: Will solar panels damage my roof?**
A: No. Our installers use roof-friendly mounting structures that distribute weight evenly. We also provide a 10-year structural warranty on all installations.
